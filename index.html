About Baler Aurora

Baler is a town on the Philippine island of Luzon. In its center, Museo de Baler chronicles colonial history. On the coast, Cemento Beach is known for powerful surf, and Sabang Beach is lined with resorts. Nearby, Ermita Hill offers panoramic views. Inland, Baler Hanging Bridge crosses the mouth of the Tibag-Sabang River. To the east, the distinctive, angular Baler Lighthouse overlooks Dicasalarin Cove.

DESTINATION

Baler is known for numerous tourist attractions. In addition to the Baler Catholic Church and other historical sites , there are resorts at Sabang Beach and Cemento Beach, where surfers from all over the world compete for the annual International Aurora Surfing Cup.

Baler Chruch

The unassuming facade of the Iglesia de San Luis Obispo de Tolosa, more popularly known as the Baler Church, belies its rich historic significance. Known as “the Last Spanish Garrison in the Philippines,” this little church was the site of the siege of Baler in the last days of the Philippine Revolution.

Zabali Hanging Bridge

Famed, long, hanging pedestrian bridge over the Tibag-Sabang River, with dramatic views.

Sabang Beach

It is a popular destination for surfers and beach-goers alike, with its pristine waters and stunning views of the Sierra Madre mountain range. Sabang Beach is known for its laid-back vibe, friendly locals, and abundance of outdoor activities.
